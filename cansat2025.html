CANSAT 2025

CANSAT project focused on small payload design, sensor integration, and real-time data telemetry for educational rocketry competitions.

Project Overview

Frequent natural disasters—such as floods, wildfires, storms, and earthquakes—pose serious risks to life and property and often make ground-based data collection and rescue operations difficult. To address these challenges, our team developed a CANSAT system to monitor toxic gas concentrations (CH4, CO2, H2S, NH3) and capture high-altitude imagery of damaged areas. We also created a supporting website to aid rescue planning, provide public information to help people avoid hazardous or polluted routes, and coordinate response efforts. Additionally, the CANSAT includes an automated parachute deployment system designed to ensure a safe, controlled descent and to activate in emergency situations or when obstacles are detected.

Mission Objectives

  • 1

    To build a CANSAT to collect environmental data, verify its position, and communicate with a ground station.

  • 2

    To develop a system that detects and reports toxic gas concentrations and captures high-altitude aerial imagery of disaster-affected areas.

  • 2

    To develop an automated parachute deployment system that activates during emergencies to ensure safe and controlled descent.
