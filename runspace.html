2025 Runspace Innovation Challenge

Cost-effective 3U CubeSat monitoring ice cap melting, greenhouse gases, sea level changes, and coastal submersion to support climate policy and global environmental collaboration.

Mission Overview

Nowadays Global warming is causing significant environmental changes, including melting ice, rising sea levels, and the slow sinking of coastlines.

These changes, driven by increasing greenhouse gas concentrations, disrupt natural systems and pose risks to both ecosystems and human settlements.

The growing demand for real-time climate data from governments, research institutions, and the private sector highlights the urgent need for better monitoring. In response, our team, CUBESIT Nong RunSpace, is developing a compact and affordable 3U CubeSat.

This mission aims to provide accessible and accurate real-time data on key environmental indicators such as ice melt, sea level rise, and greenhouse gas levels. Additionally, our team will process and analyze this data before delivering it directly to users, contributing to global climate resilience.

Key Features

  • 1

    Greenhouse gas monitoring with space-grade sensors

  • 2

    Surface temperature measurement system for polar regions

  • 3

    Data collection for ice melting analysis and sea level prediction

Technical Specifications

Design Parameters
  • • Size: 3U CubeSat
  • • Dimension: 10cm x 10cm x 30cm
  • • Weight: 5.2kg
  • • Orbit: Sun Synchronous Orbit
  • • Semimajor Axis: 6830 km
  • • Inclination: 97.2°
  • • Access Time: 4 times/day
  • • Mission Duration: 5-6 years
CubeSat Systems
  • • EPS - Electrical Power System
  • • OCB - On-Board Computer
  • • ADCS - Attitude Determination Control System
