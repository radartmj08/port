The CUBESAT Quest 2025

A competition to build a 1U satellite to carry out a specified mission, with teams competing for Her Royal Highness Queen Sirikit's Royal Trophy.

Background & Significance

The frequency and severity of natural disasters are increasing, while predictive accuracy remains challenging. Rapid monitoring, measurement, damage assessment, alerts, and rescue operations are therefore essential.

Examples: For floods (e.g., Nan Province), aerial imagery can estimate flow direction and arrival time and measure affected areas.

For collapsed buildings, aerial imagery supports damage assessment.

For chemical plant fires, thermal infrared imaging helps locate ignition points and victims, while toxic gas concentration data supports monitoring and evacuation alerts.

For tropical cyclones, humidity, temperature, and aerial imagery inform evacuation planning and post‑event damage assessments.

Objectives

  • 1

    Study and compare disaster types that may occur in Thailand.

  • 2

    Study the impacts caused by potential disasters in Thailand.

  • 3

    Build a CubeSat capable of sensing environmental conditions.

  • 4

    Build a CubeSat to capture still and video imagery and transmit data in real time.

  • 5

    Develop disaster forecasting and early‑warning systems.

  • 6

    Develop systems for rescue support and post‑disaster damage assessment.
